# Bellows — Verdict Mechanization Distribution Audit & Scope Analysis

**Priority:** 1

## Context

BACKLOG #5 frames "verdict mechanization" as Bellows-side automation of routine Rule 22 verification — letting Bellows auto-resolve `auto_close_disabled` and `qa_checkpoint` verdicts when deposit checks pass cleanly, instead of always pausing for Planner judgment. The hypothesis is that most verdicts are routine (clean Rule 22 pass) and mechanization would compress the verdict cycle.

This diagnostic does NOT assume mechanization is the right answer. It asks two prior questions: (1) what does the actual verdict outcome distribution look like in production, and (2) given that distribution, what scope of mechanization (if any) is worth building? The diagnostic must be allowed to recommend NO MECHANIZATION as a valid outcome.

The motivation for asking the prior questions traces to today's session experience and the 2026-04-09 lesson in PLANNER_TEMPLATE: "before designing governance machinery to solve a friction, diagnose where the friction actually lives." Mechanization addresses round-trip latency on routine verdicts. It does NOT address Planner-side plan-authoring bugs (e.g., today's directory-in-Deposits-block gate trip), which are the more frequent source of cycle friction in this session. If the data shows that "real" Planner findings are rarer than authoring bugs that mechanization wouldn't catch, the ROI calculus changes.

The primary data source is `bellows/verdicts/ledger.jsonl` — a JSONL audit log spanning 2026-04-16 to 2026-04-28 (~26 KB, ~14 days of dispatch history). Secondary sources include `bellows/knowledge/decisions/Done/` (~50+ plans closed via Bellows) and `bellows/knowledge/BACKLOG.md` (closed entries documenting prior verdict-related fixes).
