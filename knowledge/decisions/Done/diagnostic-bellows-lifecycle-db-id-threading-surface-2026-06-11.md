## Context (Rule 27)
Reporting Phase 1 (see `reporting-phase-0-coverage-map-2026-06-10.md` at governance root — READ IT after your specialist file; all design decisions are locked there) is the heaviest change of the reporting effort: Bellows mints a global-monotonic integer id at claim, filenames become id-only (`executable-142.md`, `diagnostic-143.md`, `verdict-142-step-2.md`), and a dedicated lifecycle DB is written at claim and updated at EVERY transition (claim → step start → gate check → verdict → close) so it serves both audit reconstruction and live status. CEO directives layered on the locked map: (1) the id is minted by Bellows at claim and the claim-time rename is the mechanism that applies it — the Planner's deposit filename becomes a transient placeholder with no durable life; (2) all parsers must tolerate BOTH the legacy slug+date format (everything in `Done/`, `halted-*`, resolved verdicts) and the new id-only format indefinitely — NO migration of historical artifacts; (3) no slug appears anywhere durable post-claim (in-progress name, verdict filenames, commit tags, Done/ filing, DB rows are all id-keyed; the descriptive name lives only in the `# Title` header and PROJECT_STATUS). This is a design diagnostic ONLY — author NO fix code, no `bellows.py`/`gates.py` edits; the implementing executable(s) come after CEO review. The riskiest seam is the mid-lifecycle filename change: everything downstream that keys on the plan filename or slug must be enumerated and proven, not assumed.
